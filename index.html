    1981

    Metallica were born on October 28, 1981 when drummer Lars Ulrich and guitar player/singer James Hetfield got together.

    East Coast metal merchant Jon Zazula pitched the band on an album deal with his indie label Megaforce Records.
    1983

    Traveling to New York in a stolen U-Haul to record their first album.

    Mustaine was replaced by Exodus shredder Kirk Hammett and the boys hit the studio for the first time. The resulting debut album, Kill ‘Em All.

    Metallica made their second album Ride The Lightning.
    1986

    Master Of Puppets, the band’s third album, which was mixed in LA with Michael Wagener and was released in March that year.

    On tour in Sweden, during an overnight drive the bands' tour bus skidded out of control and flipped, killing Cliff Burton. They tapped Jason Newsted from Flotsam & Jetsam to take over bass duties.

    The band recorded their fourth full-length album, ...And Justice For All.

    Metallica also made their very first music video for "One,” a dark, monochromatic, violent, emotional piece which underscored Metallica’s thorough uniqueness.
    1991

    Metallica released their self-titled album – better known by fans as The Black Album. It went straight to number one all over the world…and didn’t really leave for a couple of years, selling in excess of 16 million copies worldwide, spawning several legitimate singles, and earning various industry accolades, including a Grammy, MTV and American Music Awards.
